Mercury

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and is named after the Roman messenger god. It has a rocky, cratered surface and no atmosphere to speak of. It takes just 88 Earth days for Mercury to complete one orbit around the Sun.

Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty. It has a thick atmosphere made mostly of carbon dioxide and sulfuric acid clouds, which create a strong greenhouse effect that makes Venus the hottest planet in our solar system.

Earth

Earth is the third planet from the Sun and is the only known planet to support life. It has a complex atmosphere that sustains a diverse biosphere of plant and animal life, including humans. Earth has one natural satellite, the Moon, which orbits around it.

Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and is named after the Roman god of war. It has a thin atmosphere and a surface that is covered in red dust and rocks. Mars is home to the largest volcano and the deepest canyon in our solar system, and it has polar ice caps made of water and carbon dioxide.

Jupiter

Jupiter is the fifth planet from the Sun and is named after the king of the Roman gods. It is the largest planet in our solar system and is mostly made of gas, with a small rocky core. Jupiter has at least 79 known moons, including four large Galilean moons.

Saturn

Saturn is the sixth planet from the Sun and is named after the Roman god of agriculture. It is known for its beautiful rings, which are made of ice and rock particles. Saturn is also mostly made of gas, with a small rocky core. It has at least 82 known moons, including the large moon Titan.

Uranus

Uranus is the seventh planet from the Sun and is named after the Greek god of the sky. It is an ice giant planet with a blue-green color, caused by the presence of methane in its atmosphere. Uranus has at least 27 known moons and a system of rings.

Neptune

Neptune is the eighth and farthest planet from the Sun in our solar system. It is a gas giant planet with a blue color, caused by the presence of methane in its atmosphere. Neptune has a very active climate, with the fastest winds in the solar system and large storms, including the Great Dark Spot, which is similar in size to the Great Red Spot on Jupiter. Neptune has at least 14 known moons and a system of rings.
